# Assumptions Register

| date | component | assumption | rationale | impact/status |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 2026-04-02 | Source snapshot | The current `data/raw/*.xlsx` files are treated as the frozen source snapshot for Lv.5 driver alignment. | Driver alignment must be auditable against a stable source layer before cost-driver validation starts. | Active for this alignment run. |
| 2026-04-02 | Campaign mapping | `DRJ 2022`, `DRJ 2023`, and `SLK 2025` in `Data.Summary` map to official campaign codes `E530-30101-D225301`, `E530-30101-D235301`, and `E540-30101-D245401`. | `Data.Summary` uses short labels rather than official campaign names. | Approved mapping alias; hard gate for alignment. |
| 2026-04-02 | Taxonomy semantics | `hybrid` is reserved for non-well scope that is estimable from structured campaign design/scope drivers, and is no longer used as a placeholder for unknown scope. | The prior fallback blurred ambiguity with business semantics and blocked clear driver determination. | Active design rule. |
| 2026-04-02 | Material review | `supporting_cost_total >= 500000 USD` is the materiality threshold for the review queue. | High-spend keys require explicit visibility even when covered by deterministic policy. | Active review threshold. |
| 2026-04-02 | Pad scope policy | Pad-tagged road/pad, tie-in, hook-up, and new-rig-pavement families are treated as `hybrid` and routed to `pad_expansion_flag`, `tie_in_flag`, `interpad_move_count`, or `rig_skid_count` driver families as applicable. | These are not direct well costs, but they are estimable from campaign design/scope structure. | Approved curated policy. |
| 2026-04-02 | Shared support policy | Security, permits, environmental support, facilities support, water support, project management, and similar campaign-support families are treated as `campaign_tied`. | These costs are needed in campaign totals but should not be allocated to single wells in this layer. | Approved curated policy. |
| 2026-04-02 | Well-scope policy | Rig services, mud/logging/casing/cement operations, transportation, supervision, and similar operational families are treated as `well_tied` even when posted at campaign grain. | These are still conceptually direct well-cost components and should remain eligible for well-level driver validation later. | Approved curated policy. |
| 2026-04-02 | Phase 3 architecture | Field-specific processing is mandatory by design; pooled analysis is disallowed unless statistically justified and explicitly approved in the phase-exit memo. | DARAJAT and SALAK have distinct operational/cost characteristics and project guardrails require independent validation. | Active Phase 3 design control. |
| 2026-04-20 | Estimator training/backtest pool | `DRJ-STEAM 1`, `AWI 9-10`, and `AWI 9-11` are temporarily excluded from estimator and backtest pools via `data/processed/well_pool_exclusions.csv`. | `DRJ-STEAM 1` is pending canonical confirmation; `AWI 9-10` and `AWI 9-11` are pending spend completeness closeout. | Active temporary override; revisit after canonical and spend closeout evidence is available. |
| 2026-04-24 | Unit-price program scope | The dashboard-driven historical unit-price pipeline treats all seven campaigns (`DRJ - 2019`, `DRJ - 2022`, `DRJ - 2024`, `SLK - 2021`, `SLK - 2025`, `WW - 2018`, `WW - 2021`) as active canonical scope. | The user wants ground-up historical coverage and does not require the legacy `Data.Summary` pipeline to remain the sole scope gate for unit-price work. | Active for branch `data-ingestion.2.0`. |
| 2026-04-24 | Well estimator standardization | New estimated wells are treated as `Standard-J`; historical complexity remains audit context only and is not used to segment the direct well estimator. | The user explicitly removed well-complexity separation from the estimation path. | Active for unit-price well analysis and forecast path. |
| 2026-04-24 | Macro forecast weighting | Forecast weighting uses yearly nominal series correlation from 2019–2026 against oil price, CPI/inflation, and steel commodity price using Pearson's correlation. NPV/discounted treatment is diagnostic only unless later promoted by evidence. | The user explicitly requested Pearson correlation on yearly series and asked us to decide between NPV and as-is; nominal/as-is is the operational default. | Active for macro trend and forecast outputs. |
| 2026-04-24 | Steel macro proxy | The macro weighting layer uses IMF `PIORECR` iron ore annual unit price as the auditable steel commodity proxy. | A direct annual steel-HRC series was not available in the official annual IMF/WB files used for the yearly 2019–2026 analysis, but iron ore is the closest transparent steel-input series available in the same source family. | Active for macro correlation and forecast weighting until a better official annual steel series is added. |
| 2026-04-24 | Macro support gating | Macro weights are operational only at pooled `pricing_basis` level; field-specific yearly Pearson rows remain diagnostic until each field has at least four overlap years. | Current field-specific yearly history is too thin for statistically credible Pearson weighting, but pooled pricing-basis rows still provide an auditable external-adjustment layer without pooling the base field cost benchmarks. | Active for macro output interpretation and estimator wiring. |
| 2026-04-24 | Service-time band method | `Fast`, `Standard`, and `Careful` are derived per field from terciles of `active_operational_days per 1000 ft` using the dashboard well history context. | The user asked for statistically backed field-specific ranges, and terciles give an auditable, non-arbitrary split without reintroducing complexity classes. | Active for standard-well time-band references. |
| 2026-04-24 | NPT contribution scope | NPT driver ranking is field-level, using event-major/detail duration shares and per-well correlation to total NPT. Field is imputed from context or well-name prefix when campaign is unavailable, and WW remains an explicit no-event-context gap. | The local event dataset is materially richer at field level than at campaign level, and the user asked for contributor ranking plus penalty support rather than a campaign-only audit. | Active for NPT contribution and penalty outputs. |
